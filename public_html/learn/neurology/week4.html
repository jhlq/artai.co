Week 4

Important concepts

1. Glutamate receptors

  • Agonist: Activator of receptors.
  • Antagonist: Blocker of receptors.
  • Desensitization: Decreased response of a receptor after several activations.
  • Ligand-gated: Activation depends on a molecular attachment at the side.

2. Postsynaptic potentials

  • EPSC: Excitatory postsynaptic current
  • EPSP: Excitatory postsynaptic potential

3. Glutamatergic circuits

  • Tactile: Relating to touch.

4. Synaptic plasticity

  • Plasticity: Ability to change.
  • LTP: Long term potentiation, increase in transmission probability.
  • LTD: Long term depression, decrease in transmission probability.

5. Dendritic spines

  • Motile: Able to move.
  • Filament: Threadlike structure.
  • Unit fl: Femtolitre, femto is 10^-15

  • After an EPSP, the membrane potential discharges with the membrane time constant. In EPSCs the membrane potential is clamped, therefore there is no discharge, decay time is only dependent on the ion-channel kinetics.

  • During an EPSC, the presynaptic terminal stops releasing neurotransmitter much earlier than during an EPSP. This causes a shorter time constant of decay for EPSCs.
